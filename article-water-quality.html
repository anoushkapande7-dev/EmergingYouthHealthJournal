Water Quality Analysis of Public Sites in New Jersey

Student Research Article • Public Health

Abstract

This study evaluates water quality across public locations in New Jersey using standardized chemical testing methods to assess safety and environmental consistency.

Methods

Water samples were collected from five public sites and analyzed using test kits measuring pH, hardness, and chlorine concentration.

Results

All samples fell within safe drinking water limits, with minor variation in mineral composition across locations.

Conclusion

Overall, public water quality in the tested areas is safe, though variation suggests differences in treatment processes.
